Your first weapon

After the tutorial with Marcia Ramirez you can choose a starting weapon. Melee options are solid for early game—pick one and stick with it until you unlock more.

Basic combat tips

Stay mobile, watch your stamina, and use the environment. Crowds can overwhelm you quickly; pull enemies when you can.

Early quest order

Do Humble Beginnings for Eli, then pick up other starter quests in Junction. Aim for level 4 to unlock more NPCs and quests.

One-handed melee

Fast attacks, good for kiting. Lower damage per hit but safer playstyle.

Two-handed melee

Slower and heavier hits. Better for burst damage and clearing single targets.

Ranged weapons

Unlocked later. Ammo and durability matter—save for tougher fights when possible.

Unlocking the safehouse

Complete the relevant quest in Junction to gain access. It becomes your base for storage and crafting.

Storage and stash

Use the stash to keep items safe. Organize by type so you can find gear and materials quickly.

Basic crafting

Crafting stations are inside the safehouse. Gather materials in the world and bring them back to craft gear and consumables.

Choosing a profession

Professions grant unique recipes and bonuses. Pick one that fits your playstyle—you can specialise later.

Gathering and crafting

Some professions focus on gathering, others on crafting. Both are useful for progression and economy.

Faction overview

Factions offer unique quests, rewards, and reputation. Your choices can lock or unlock content.

Reputation and rewards

Build reputation by doing faction quests and tasks. Higher reputation unlocks better rewards and access.

Endgame activities

Higher-level zones, dungeons, and repeatable content open up. Focus on gear, professions, and faction goals.

Tips for late game

Optimise your build, keep your safehouse upgraded, and stay on good terms with at least one faction for best rewards.
